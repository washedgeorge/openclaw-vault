1915—February High 83c. The third time at this level. February
opposite the month for seasonal change, showed High at a time

1915—October Low 55c per bushel. Holding above 50c a bushel was a
sign of strength and making Tops in October, the month for
seasonal Lows, was important and you should have bought
because war has a bullish effect on prices of corn and everything
else. Advance followed.

1916—March High 81%. A still lower Tops than February, 1915. A
selling level. A reaction followed.

1916—May Low 63c per bushel. After a series of Bottoms and Tops
around this level. This was a natural half-way point or support
level and a place to buy, especially as prices were running
opposite seasonal trends, making lows in the month of May when
most Highs are made.
Prices holding above the Lows of 1915, indicated strength. Half-

was crossed and prices indicated higher. For the fourth time

the same Tops the fourth time they nearly always go through
and they did go through with a rapid move upwards. Prices
