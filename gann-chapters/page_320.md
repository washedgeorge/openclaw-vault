1931—-August, HIGH 165. Under the OLD BOTTOM LEVELS and
a LOWER TOP. Still a short sale and the main trend was down.
A long, slow decline followed.

1932—-June, FINAL LOW 60. This was 60/100 of a cent, or just a
little above 14c per pound. Note that the 75% decline from 240
to 60 held for three months, making bottoms around this ex-
tremely low figure.

1932—September, HIGH 120. A 100% advance on 60. After the first
advance from the extreme low levels there is always a
SECONDARY RALLY.

1933—February, LOW 72. This was a SECONDARY RALLY and a
HIGHER BOTTOM. The market held for two months in a
range of less than 30 points. Then the main trend turned up
and prices crossed 120. They were above six months tops and
over 100% on the bottom level.

1933—July, HIGH 185. This was a 200% advance on the LOW LEVEL
and up under old bottom levels and around old top levels. A
decline followed.

1933—October, LOW 125. Holding above the 100% advance on the
low level and at a place where there was a series of bottoms. A
BUYING LEVEL. Prices worked higher.
