1898—May, high, 185. This was the Leiter torner. Note that 182
was 7%, of 200 to 49. Armour brought about the collapse of the
market and failure of Leiter by shipping wheat into Chicago by
express trains. The decline was rapid, as all of these declines
are when the market is cornered, and the man who tries to corner
it goes broke, and cannot get out. Here again we figure from the
extreme low of 49c per bushel to 185, which gives $1.17 per
