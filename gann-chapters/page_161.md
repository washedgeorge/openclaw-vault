1869—January Low 44 cents. Down near the 1/3 point of 1.40 per
bushel. Bottom was made here and this was a Buying Level.
The market was stronger when Corn crossed 50 cents per bushel.

1869—August High 9714 cents per bushel. Failing to reach 1.00 per
bushel was an indication of weakness and made this a Selling
Level. August is the seasonal month for change in trend when
most Tops are reached. Therefore, this would have been the
place to sell out and sell short.
