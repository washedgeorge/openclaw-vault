# Key Research Papers — Washed's Health Stack

Papers directly relevant to insulin resistance, mitochondrial dysfunction, muscle rebuilding, and peptide stack.

## MOTS-C

**Lee C et al. (2015)** "The mitochondrial-derived peptide MOTS-c promotes metabolic homeostasis and reduces obesity and insulin resistance" — *Cell Metabolism* — PMID: 25738459
- Discovery paper. MOTS-c is a 16-amino-acid peptide encoded in mitochondrial 12S rRNA
- Primary target: skeletal muscle
- Mechanism: inhibits folate cycle → blocks de novo purine biosynthesis → activates AMPK
- In mice: prevented age-dependent AND high-fat-diet-induced insulin resistance + obesity
- Conclusion: mitochondria actively regulate metabolic homeostasis via peptides

**Bhullar KS et al. (2021)** "Mitofusion is required for MOTS-c induced GLUT4 translocation" — *Scientific Reports* — PMID: 34253808
- MOTS-c increases mitochondrial biogenesis markers (TFAM, COX4, NRF1, PGC-1α)
- Activates mitochondrial fusion via OPA1 and MFN2 GTPases
- Mitofusion is REQUIRED for GLUT4 translocation — block fusion, block glucose uptake
- First paper linking mitochondrial fusion to insulin-independent glucose uptake

**Reynolds JC et al. (2021)** "MOTS-c is an exercise-induced mitochondrial-encoded regulator of age-dependent physical decline" — *Nature Communications*
- Proved MOTS-c levels increase with exercise
- Translocates to nucleus under metabolic stress to regulate gene expression
- Confirms exercise requirement for full activation

## Retatrutide

**Rosenstock J et al. (2023)** "Retatrutide, a GIP, GLP-1 and glucagon receptor agonist, for people with type 2 diabetes" — *Lancet* — PMID: 37385280
- Phase 2, 42 centers in USA
- Triple agonist: GIP + GLP-1 + glucagon
- At 12mg dose: HbA1c reduced by -2.02%, body weight reduced by -16.94%
- Dose-dependent weight loss across all dose groups
- Significant improvement in glycemic control AND body weight simultaneously

**Jastreboff AM et al. (2023)** "Retatrutide for Obesity" — *NEJM*
- Phase 2 obesity trial: -24.2% body weight at 48 weeks (highest dose)
- Key: glucagon agonism increases energy expenditure (BMR boost)
- Visceral fat reduction significant

## Insulin Resistance (Foundational)

**Petersen MC & Shulman GI (2018)** "Mechanisms of Insulin Action and Insulin Resistance" — *Physiological Reviews*
- THE definitive paper on insulin resistance mechanisms
- DAGs (diacylglycerols) activate PKCε in liver and PKCθ in muscle → block insulin receptor substrate phosphorylation
- Ceramides activate PP2A and PKCζ → block Akt2 signaling
- Intracellular lipid accumulation is the ROOT CAUSE, not circulating glucose
- Gerald Shulman (Yale) = leading authority on this mechanism

**Samuel VT & Shulman GI (2012)** "Mechanisms for Insulin Resistance" — *Cell*
- Hepatic vs muscle insulin resistance: different lipid mediators, same outcome
- Muscle IR: reduced glucose uptake → hyperglycemia
- Liver IR: increased gluconeogenesis → more glucose output
- Both driven by ectopic lipid accumulation

## Tesamorelin

**Falutz J et al. (2007)** "Metabolic effects of GHRH in patients with HIV" — *NEJM*
- FDA approval study. 2mg/day subQ.
- Reduced trunk fat by 15.2% vs placebo
- Improved lipid profiles
- IGF-1 increased to normal physiological range

**Stanley TL et al. (2014)** "Effects of tesamorelin on NAFLD in HIV" — *Gut*
- Reduced hepatic fat fraction
- Improved hepatic insulin sensitivity

## Berberine (for HOMA-IR)

**Lan J et al. (2015)** "Meta-analysis of berberine for type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, hypertension" — 28 RCTs
- Reduced HOMA-IR by ~0.5-0.9
- Comparable efficacy to metformin in some head-to-head comparisons
- Mechanism: AMPK activation (same pathway as MOTS-c)

## BPC-157

**Seiwerth S et al. (2018)** "BPC 157's effect on healing" — *J Physiol Pharmacol*
- Comprehensive review of wound/tissue healing mechanisms
- Promotes angiogenesis (new blood vessel growth) via VEGF
- Anti-inflammatory properties
- Tendon, ligament, muscle, and bone healing demonstrated in animal models

## Cold Exposure + Brown Fat

**Kang GS et al. (2023)** "Sensing oxygen and temperature in adipose tissues" — *Exp Mol Med* — PMID: 37907745
- Cold activates UCP1 in brown adipose tissue
- Uncouples mitochondria → burns glucose/fatty acids for heat
- Improves insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism

## Time-Restricted Eating

**Wong PS et al. (2025)** "16/8 TRE on Glucose Metabolism" — *Nutrition Reviews* — PMID: 41351878
- Meta-analysis: 16/8 TRE reduced fasting glucose (SMD -0.25, p=0.004), HOMA-IR (SMD -0.16, p=0.03)
- Mild but consistent benefits across multiple populations

## Protein + Muscle Preservation in Deficit

**Morton RW et al. (2018)** "A systematic review of protein intake and resistance training" — *Br J Sports Med*
- Muscle protein synthesis maxes out at ~1.6g/kg/day
- Beyond this, diminishing returns
- Combined with resistance training, adequate protein preserves lean mass even in caloric deficit
